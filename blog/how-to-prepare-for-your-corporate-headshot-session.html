How to Prepare for Your Corporate Headshot Session

A professional corporate headshot is more than just a photo—it's an investment in your personal brand and professional future. Here's your comprehensive guide to ensuring you get the most out of your headshot session.

Wardrobe Selection

Choose clothing that reflects your industry and personal brand while remaining timeless. Solid colors typically photograph best, with deep blues, grays, and neutral tones being particularly effective. Avoid busy patterns or trendy styles that may date quickly. Bring multiple options to your session—typically 2-3 different looks allow for variety while maintaining efficiency.

Grooming and Preparation

Schedule any haircuts 1-2 weeks before your session to allow your style to settle naturally. For makeup, opt for a natural, polished look that enhances rather than transforms. Men should consider a fresh shave or beard trim the morning of the shoot. Get adequate rest the night before to ensure you look refreshed and energetic.

Posing Tips

While your photographer will guide you through poses, understanding basic principles can help you feel more confident. Practice maintaining good posture and experiment with angles in your mirror. Remember to relax your shoulders, keep your chin slightly down and forward, and maintain a natural, approachable expression.

Mental Preparation

Approach your session with confidence and openness. Your photographer is there to help you look your best. Consider the message you want your headshot to convey and the audience you're trying to reach. This mental clarity will help inform your expression and overall presence in the photos.

Technical Considerations

Understanding how your headshot will be used can influence your preparation. If it's primarily for LinkedIn, consider the platform's square format. For website use, you might need space around your head for text overlay. Communicate these needs to your photographer before the session.

Day-of Tips

  • Arrive 10-15 minutes early to settle in and review your options with the photographer
  • Bring a small grooming kit for touch-ups
  • Stay hydrated and avoid heavy meals just before the session
  • Bring reference photos of styles you like
  • Remember any specific requirements from your company or industry

After the Session

Review your images thoughtfully, considering how they align with your professional goals. Don't rush the selection process—take time to gather feedback from trusted colleagues if needed. Remember that your headshot is often the first impression you make in the professional world, so choose an image that authentically represents you while maintaining professional standards.
